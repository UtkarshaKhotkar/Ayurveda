Tips with Ayurveda

Everyday strategies to improve your life

Here below is a collection of everyday tips and strategies to improve your health and lifestyle, drawing upon timeless Ayurvedic principles and modern science. The purpose of this material is to empower every reader to make incremental changes to the way we eat, sleep, exercise, work, communicate and engage with this thing called "life". Ayurveda, after all, means The Science of Life. In addition to Ayurveda tips, there are write-ups on various topics that help us to understand the philosophy of Ayurveda, what it has to offer and how it can enrich our lives

Here are some simple ayurvedic practices to perform for a healthy lifestyle:
  • Morning routine(Dinacharya): Tongue scraping, Oil pulling, Drinking warm water with lemon
  • Healthy eating habits: Eating according to body type or dosa(Vata, pitta or kapha), paying attention to meal time and eating mindfully.
  • Yoga and pranayama for good posture and breathing, also for a balanced mind and body.
  • Daily exercises can include walking, cycling or swimming for a refreshed body and mind.
  • Adequate sleep and rest play a crucial role in the overall health of a person. A sleep schedule needs to be followed.
  • Stress management can be done in any way like meditation, walking, self-care routines, etc. One must have a stress management routine.
  • Herbal remedies are a great way for starting the day with a fresh mind and body. A herbal tea with tulsi, ashwagandha, neem , adrak(ginger), etc can make one's body nourished as well as purified.
  • Hydration is necessary for one's body. Having plenty of water purifies the body and flushes out the toxins leaving the body hydrated.
  • Mindful eating is an important habit for one to have. Being aware about what you eat, how you eat, how many times and how you chew the food is a need.
  • Self massage(Abhyanga): Regular self-massages can improve blood circulation and provide relaxation to tensed muscles. Massages need to be done in circular motions on the tensed areas of the body.
